Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a reference-free topic segmentation metric named "SegReFree," which does not rely on reference or human-labeled segmentations. The metric is based on the Davies-Bouldin Index, modified for sequence segmentation by comparing only adjacent segments and penalizing intra-segment similarity scores exponentially based on segment size. The authors compute embeddings for each sentence using Sentence-BERT and demonstrate strong correlations between SegReFree and existing metrics across various error types on two topic segmentation datasets.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The proposed metric shows strong correlation with existing segmentation metrics for most error types and is useful for evaluating segmentation without reference segmentations.
- The motivation and design of the metric are clearly articulated, and the paper is well-written with a comprehensive introduction and related work.

Weaknesses:
- The modifications to the Davies-Bouldin Index are relatively minor and straightforward.
- The paper lacks comparisons with newer techniques using embeddings and does not clarify how it differs from prior work by Solbiati et al. (2021) and Ghinassi (2021).
- Several figures and cross-references are missing or incorrectly referenced, which detracts from the presentation.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of the experiments by providing clearer explanations of metrics such as 1-seg-sim and 1-bound-sim, and the overall takeaways from the figures. Additionally, addressing the missing figures and correcting cross-references will enhance the paper's presentation. It would also be beneficial to compare the proposed metric with newer techniques and clarify its distinctions from existing work. Finally, consider consolidating similar figures and tables to streamline the presentation.
