Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents STGAN, a spatio-temporal graph-based anomaly detection model aimed at identifying advanced cyberattacks using Host Provenance Graphs (HPGs). The authors propose integrating spatial, temporal, and semantic features to enhance detection accuracy. The framework is well-structured and demonstrates strong performance on benchmark datasets, surpassing existing methods. However, the evaluation is limited to a narrow set of datasets, raising concerns about generalizability and scalability.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
1. The integration of spatio-temporal features significantly improves host-based threat detection performance, showcasing innovation.
2. Experimental results indicate that STGAN outperforms existing approaches, suggesting potential for real-world applications.
3. The paper includes a thorough modular analysis and sensitivity testing, validating the effectiveness of each component.

Weaknesses:
1. The model's training on only three benchmark datasets may lead to overfitting, limiting generalization to unseen data. Additional diverse datasets are recommended for training and evaluation.
2. There is insufficient assessment of practical applicability, particularly regarding parameter size, FLOPs, and computational time costs, which are crucial for real-world performance.
3. The computational complexity introduced by multiple encoders and the self-attention mechanism is not analyzed, leaving questions about detection latency and resource usage.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve scalability testing by incorporating additional diverse datasets for training and evaluation to enhance robustness. It is essential to provide more implementation details, including real-world case studies, and to expand the discussion on limitations. Furthermore, we suggest addressing practical scaling limitations, real-time detection latency, and the impact of batch size on detection time versus accuracy trade-offs. Lastly, the authors should clarify the training data requirements and model update strategies for deployment, as well as explore data reduction techniques or optimizations in graph neural networks to mitigate computational strain.
