Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a novel approach to Early Classification of Time Series (ECTS) by addressing the Pareto optimality constraint, which has not been previously explored. The authors propose a method that minimizes both false positives and decision lateness while adhering to a minimal sensitivity constraint. They demonstrate the existence of an optimal solution and provide a proof of concept using a recurrent neural network (RNN) trained on this objective. The paper includes multiple experiments across various data types, ensuring statistical significance and empirical robustness.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The approach utilizes the Neyman-Pearson paradigm, advancing existing frameworks both experimentally and theoretically.
- Extensive experimentation with repeated trials enhances the robustness of the findings.

Weaknesses:
- The poster's structure complicates the reader's ability to follow the work, particularly due to heavy notation without a clear subsection for definitions.
- Important elements, such as Proposition A.1 and details in Appendix B, are relegated to the appendix, making it difficult for readers to grasp essential theoretical components.
- The paper's non-Bayesian approach may not align well with the workshop's focus on Bayesian decision-making, limiting its acceptance.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the poster's structure by including a dedicated subsection for notation to aid reader comprehension. Additionally, we suggest integrating crucial elements from the appendix into the main text to provide clarity on key concepts like Pareto optimality and the Plug In to the RNN. Furthermore, we encourage the authors to address the non-Bayesian nature of their approach in relation to the workshop's theme. Lastly, we advise enhancing the language editing throughout the paper to eliminate grammatical errors and improve readability.
