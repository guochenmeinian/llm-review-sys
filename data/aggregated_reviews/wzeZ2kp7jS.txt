Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents an LLM-based industry categorization system aimed at IP-level categorization of IP addresses, resulting in the creation of the IPdb dataset containing 200 million IP addresses. The proposed model consists of four components: domain identification using DNS PTR records, organization identification via WHOIS lookups, web description collection from Wikipedia, and organization classification through ensembled LLM models with dynamic ensembling weights. The authors highlight the implications of their findings on web service deployment strategies across various industries.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The paper contributes a significant dataset (IPdb) and a high-precision LLM-augmented classification process.
- Experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the robustness of the proposed methods.
- The writing is clear, and the methodology is well-structured, addressing a practical challenge in the field.

Weaknesses:
- The methodology relies on existing technologies without clear innovations, raising questions about the novelty of the approach.
- Some abbreviations are introduced without explanation, potentially confusing readers.
- The related work section is too concise and lacks detailed comparisons with recent advancements, which may undermine the paper's context and relevance.
- The gold standard dataset used for evaluation is relatively small, which may affect the generalizability of the framework.
- The model's recall rate indicates that many relevant classifications are missed, which could impact applications requiring comprehensive coverage.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of abbreviations by providing definitions upon their first appearance. Additionally, we suggest expanding the related work section to include more detailed comparisons with recent techniques and to position it earlier in the paper for better logical flow. To enhance the generalizability of the proposed framework, consider increasing the size of the gold standard dataset. Furthermore, the authors should address the uncertainties in LLM outputs and discuss how to quantify the uncertainty behind label annotations. Finally, insights into the implications of misclassifications and the impact of AS usage on cybersecurity and regulatory compliance would strengthen the paper's contributions.
