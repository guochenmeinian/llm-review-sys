Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents FP-Rainbow, a systematic approach to explore the mapping between configuration parameters of Chromium-based web browsers and their fingerprints. The authors analyze 1,748 configuration parameters, identifying that 37-61 of them significantly affect the Browser Object Model (BOM). By collecting over 61,000 fingerprints from various Chromium versions, they demonstrate the ability to identify configuration parameters from unknown fingerprints with an average accuracy of 84%. The study emphasizes the importance of understanding how configuration parameters impact browser security and user privacy.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
1. The study provides a comprehensive analysis of configuration parameters affecting browser fingerprints, revealing critical settings.
2. FP-Rainbow effectively identifies configuration parameters from unknown fingerprints, showcasing high accuracy rates.
3. The methodology reduces the need for extensive datasets across multiple devices, enhancing efficiency.

Weaknesses:
1. The dataset was generated in a Docker environment, which may not accurately reflect real-world browser behavior, limiting the generalizability of findings.
2. The study primarily focuses on Chromium, raising questions about the scalability of the methodology to other browsers.
3. The paper lacks sufficient background and technical details, making it difficult to follow, particularly regarding the definitions of attributes and switches.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of the paper by providing more detailed explanations of the background concepts, particularly the definitions of attributes and switches. Additionally, a thorough evaluation of FP-Rainbow against existing anti-fingerprinting tools is necessary to assess its effectiveness. We suggest expanding the analysis of multi-switch fingerprints and including more hardware platforms beyond Linux to enhance the significance of the results. Furthermore, the authors should clarify the implications of identifying configuration parameters from fingerprints and discuss the potential vulnerabilities that could arise. Lastly, addressing the limitations of the Docker environment and exploring the impact of various operating systems would strengthen the paper's applicability.
