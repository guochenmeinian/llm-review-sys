Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents an analysis of language models' performance on sentiment analysis tasks, focusing on the identification of "sentiment direction" in hidden representations and the concept of "summarization motifs." The authors find that sentiment information is encoded linearly and stored in non-semantic tokens, such as punctuation marks, which serve as placeholders for further processing. The findings are supported by extensive experimentation, including correlational and causal representational experiments.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The paper employs an incremental analysis method that yields interesting results.
- It introduces novel findings in sentiment analysis interpretability.
- The experimentation is rigorous and well-designed, with effective triangulation of sentiment representation across different datasets.

Weaknesses:
- The narrative becomes less coherent starting from section 4, resembling a list of results without a unifying thread.
- The connection between world models and sentiment in language models is unclear, and the suggestion that LMs track entities and states lacks sufficient evidence.
- The term "summarization motif" may not be the most appropriate, as it requires clarification to avoid confusion with other phenomena.
- The discussion on negation appears unmotivated and lacks clarity.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the coherence of the narrative in the latter sections of the paper to maintain a clear thread throughout. Additionally, we suggest clarifying the connection between world models and sentiment representation, and providing a caveat regarding the linearity of sentiment representation. The term "summarization motif" should be reconsidered for clarity, and the authors should better motivate the discussion on negation. Furthermore, we advise enhancing the editing of the second half of the paper, addressing issues such as inconsistent formatting, reliance on color in figures, and the presentation of tables that may be indecipherable.
