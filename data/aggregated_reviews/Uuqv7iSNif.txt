Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a novel investigation into ASR error correction for code-switching contexts, specifically Chinese-English dialogues. The authors create two new datasets, SEAME-C and ASCEND-C, by pairing ground truth transcripts with ASR hypotheses from a finetuned multilingual ASR model (XLSR-53). They propose a Controllable Iterative (CI) data augmentation method that combines rule-based and machine learning approaches to generate synthetic training data, demonstrating improvements over baseline methods.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The investigation into ASR error correction in code-switching is novel and well-executed.
- Insightful data analysis guides the synthetic data generation process.
- The proposed CI data augmentation method is well-motivated and effectively designed.
- Comprehensive details on dataset creation and experimental setup are provided.

Weaknesses:
- The training data for baseline comparisons is inadequately explained, raising concerns about the fairness of comparisons.
- The generalizability of results beyond the XLSR-53 model is unclear, limiting the applicability of the proposed datasets and methods.
- Important methodological details regarding data construction and augmentation are insufficiently described, affecting reproducibility.
- The paper lacks a discussion of error statistics across different ASR systems, relying solely on outputs from a single ASR model.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve clarity on the training data used for baseline comparisons and provide more details on the datasets' construction and augmentation methods. It would be beneficial to include additional evaluations on other ASR models to assess generalizability. We suggest moving critical information from the Appendix to the main text to enhance reproducibility. Additionally, addressing the questions posed in the reviews regarding metrics, model configurations, and error analysis would strengthen the paper. Finally, including a discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed method could provide valuable insights.
