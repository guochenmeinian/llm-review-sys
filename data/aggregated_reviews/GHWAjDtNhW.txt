Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents the Latent Spatial Dirichlet Allocation (LSDA) method, which integrates Gaussian Processes (GPs) into the traditional Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) model to capture spatial structures in data. The authors develop efficient approximations for posterior inference and apply the model to cancer multiplex imaging data and synthetic datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:  
- The paper is well-written, with a clear explanation of the methodology.  
- The application of the proposed method to two distinct cases showcases its potential.  
- The method is comprehensible, supported by details in the appendix, and presents an interesting approach to sampling alpha values for spatial Dirichlet allocation.

Weaknesses:  
- The authors do not compare the model's performance with baseline models such as LDA and Spatial-LDA when applied to cancer multiplex imaging data, which would highlight the proposed model's advantages.  
- The performance on synthetic data may not be entirely fair, as the generative model for the synthetic data is identical to the proposed model. Additional evaluations on varied synthetic datasets are needed.  
- Figures 1a and 2 contain readability issues due to small font and image sizes, and there is a lack of clarity regarding the determination of the number of segments.  
- The paper lacks a limitations section and does not clarify how the spatial Dirichlet allocation process differs from existing models.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the comparison of their model with baseline models such as LDA and Spatial-LDA to better illustrate its advantages. Additionally, conducting evaluations on diverse synthetic datasets would strengthen their claims. Increasing the font size in Figure 1a and the images in Figure 2 would enhance readability. Including illustrations of samples from the GP prior in the appendix would provide helpful visual context. We also suggest clarifying how the number of segments is determined and whether the GP is optimized during LSDA model training. Learning parameters $a$ and $b$ could enhance the model's applicability across various datasets. Finally, adding a limitations section and clarifying the differences between their spatial Dirichlet allocation process and existing models would improve the paper's clarity and depth.
