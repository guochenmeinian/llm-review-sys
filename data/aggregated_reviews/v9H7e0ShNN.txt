Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents the Unified Geometry Knowledge Graph Embedding (UniGE), which integrates Euclidean and hyperbolic spaces to address the challenges of diverse topological structures in knowledge graphs. The authors propose a novel embedding alignment method and fusion strategy utilizing optimal transport techniques and Wasserstein barycenter methods. The theoretical analysis supports the effectiveness of UniGE, and experimental evaluations demonstrate its performance on benchmark datasets, albeit with only slight improvements over existing methods.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The work introduces a novel approach that synthesizes Euclidean and hyperbolic spaces for knowledge graph representation.
- The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, supporting the proposed methods effectively.
- The experimental results indicate that the synthesis of embedding spaces has practical benefits.

Weaknesses:
- The theoretical presentation may be too dense for some readers, potentially hindering accessibility.
- The performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods are modest and lack statistical validation.
- The paper does not provide sufficient implementation details or a cost-time analysis compared to existing methods.
- The writing style is occasionally hyperbolic, with claims of being "groundbreaking" that may not align with the observed results.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of the theoretical aspects to enhance accessibility for a broader audience. Additionally, providing more detailed comparisons with existing methods, including quantitative results and a discussion of advantages and limitations, would strengthen the evaluation. We suggest including a cost-time analysis of the UniGE approach and elaborating on the motivation for using optimal transport techniques and Wasserstein barycenter methods. Furthermore, addressing the omission of other relevant approaches, such as deep learning-based methods and GIE, in the related work section would provide a more comprehensive evaluation. Lastly, we advise the authors to tone down self-congratulatory language to maintain a more objective tone in presenting their contributions.
