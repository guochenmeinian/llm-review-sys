Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a graph diffusion model with fairness correction for graph generation and link prediction. The authors introduce a graph regularizer for fairness based on theoretical bounds for subgroup distance and representation distance, which is a novel contribution. The model is empirically tested, demonstrating improved fairness with a marginal reduction in accuracy.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:  
- The method performs well within its intended contributions, and the paper is well-written with clear presentation.  
- The theoretical results are satisfactory, correctly motivating the method.  
- The work is original in its approach to fairness in graph learning, supported by thorough experiments and high-quality writing.

Weaknesses:  
- There is a lack of discussion on existing work, particularly regarding the novelty of the theoretical analysis, as it overlooks relevant references.  
- Clarity and presentation issues exist, with specific questions raised about the experiments and definitions used in the paper.  
- The fairness definition lacks convincing justification, as it frames a common social phenomenon as inherently problematic without sufficient discussion.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the discussion of existing work, particularly referencing relevant studies such as ref [24] and "Debiasing Graph Representations via Metadata-Orthogonal Training" (doi/10.1109/ASONAM49781.2020.9381348). Additionally, addressing the clarity issues raised in the questions section is crucial. Specifically, clarify which link prediction model was trained on the generated graphs and whether FairWire regularization was applied. We suggest visualizing the generated graphs more effectively and providing more intuition regarding the ignored fairness criterion $\alpha_2$. Finally, consider including more representative backbone generation models in the experiments to solidify the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
