Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents GraphInflu, a novel method aimed at Few-Shot Graph Domain Adaptation (FSGDA) by optimizing graph neural network training in target domains with limited labeled data. The framework includes a Supportive Node Selector that identifies influential source nodes and a Soft Logic-Inspired Node Reweighting module that enhances the importance of high-value nodes. The authors conduct extensive experiments demonstrating that GraphInflu outperforms existing baselines in FSGDA scenarios.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The authors uncover the negative impact of adverse source nodes in scenarios with scarce annotated target nodes.
- The proposed method effectively filters out harmful nodes while enhancing high-value nodes, showcasing its innovative approach.
- Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness and superiority of the method.

Weaknesses:
- The related work section lacks analysis of recent advancements, relying on outdated comparative methods from 2022.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to three citation networks, with commonly used datasets like Cora, Cornell, and Arxiv absent.
- The methodology introduces numerous approximations, leading to higher complexity and reduced scalability.
- Clarity issues arise from grammatical errors and excessive notation in figures, affecting readability.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the related work section by incorporating a discussion of more recent and representative studies in the field. Additionally, expanding the dataset range beyond citation networks would enhance the generalizability of the approach. To clarify the differences between the proposed node re-weighting strategy and StucRW (ICML23), the authors should provide a detailed comparison. Furthermore, including results for scenarios using 100% of the source nodes would clarify the method's effectiveness in the presence of adverse nodes. Lastly, addressing grammatical issues and reducing notation complexity in figures will improve the paper's overall readability.
