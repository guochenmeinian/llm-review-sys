Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a study on low-rank matrix recovery within the semi-random model, where a subset of measurement matrices satisfies the Restricted Isometry Property (RIP) while others are chosen adversarially. The authors propose an iterative re-weighted algorithm that aims to recover the ground truth matrix, extending previous work on sparse vector recovery to the matrix setting. The theoretical guarantees provided are robust, but the practical implications and empirical validation are lacking.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The paper effectively connects the sparse recovery framework to low-rank matrix recovery, providing solid theoretical results.
- It addresses a significant theoretical problem in machine learning with implications for robustness in low-rank signal recovery.
- The introduction of novel weighted and relaxed RIP conditions is a meaningful contribution.

Weaknesses:
- The practical motivation for the semi-random model is insufficiently developed.
- There is a notable absence of empirical evaluation, particularly on synthetic data.
- Some theoretical claims remain unproven, particularly regarding improvements over existing non-convex relaxations and the implications of local minima in the semi-random model.
- The algorithm's complexity is higher than that of existing non-convex methods in the random model.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the practical motivation for the semi-random model within the paper. Additionally, including experimental results on synthetic data would strengthen the theoretical claims and demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness. The authors should provide proofs for the unverified theoretical claims regarding non-convex relaxations and clarify the computational complexity in relation to existing methods. Furthermore, enhancing the explanation of the weight oracle and its role in the algorithm, particularly in relation to lemma 4.2, would provide valuable intuition. Lastly, the authors should explicitly state the limitations of their work as required by the guidelines.
