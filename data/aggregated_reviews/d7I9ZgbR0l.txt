Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a novel framework for automatic product tagging in e-commerce, generating "Behavior-enhanced Tags" (BETags) by refining initial tags created by large language models (LLMs) with user behavior data. The authors propose a three-step approach: generating initial tags from item metadata, fine-tuning the LLM with user interaction sequences, and employing multi-beam search to produce diverse tags. Extensive experiments on three datasets demonstrate that BETags outperform both human-annotated tags and prior automated methods, enhancing search optimization and product discoverability.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The integration of user behavior data with LLMs enhances the contextual relevance and personalization of item tags.
- Strong experimental validation shows BETags outperforming state-of-the-art methods across multiple datasets.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear evidence of performance improvements.

Weaknesses:
- Limited baseline comparisons, as the paper does not evaluate against other state-of-the-art retrieval and recommendation models that do not rely on tagging.
- The model's robustness to data sparsity and noise is not addressed, raising concerns about its performance with incomplete or inconsistent user behavior data.
- The implementation code is not provided, which raises reproducibility concerns.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the paper by including comparisons with additional baseline models, such as Recformer and CALRec, to strengthen claims of BETagâ€™s effectiveness. Additionally, the authors should address the robustness of BETag in scenarios with sparse or noisy user behavior data and clarify whether specific components are designed to handle these challenges. Finally, providing the implementation code or detailed steps for reproducibility would enhance the paper's technical quality and allow for independent validation of the findings.
