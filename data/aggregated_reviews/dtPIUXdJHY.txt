Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a novel vector contraction inequality and derives tighter generalization bounds for Label-Specific Representation Learning (LSRL) in multi-label learning. The authors propose bounds with a weaker dependency on the number of labels than existing theories, enhancing the theoretical understanding of LSRL. The analysis includes three typical LSRL methods, revealing the impact of different label-specific representations on generalization.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:  
1. The introduction of a new vector contraction inequality provides a theoretical framework that was previously lacking.  
2. The derived generalization bounds offer a better dependency on the number of labels, enhancing theoretical guarantees.  
3. The paper effectively analyzes the generalization bounds for three typical LSRL methods, contributing to a deeper understanding of multi-label learning.  
4. The assumptions made are reasonable and aligned with real scenarios, with a coherent reasoning process.

Weaknesses:  
1. The derivation of the general bound is too brief, lacking intuitive descriptions that could aid understanding.  
2. The paper assumes a fixed structure for the DNN-based LSRL method, which may introduce bias; a broader analysis of network structures is needed.  
3. The theoretical analyses are limited to a few label-specific representations, potentially lacking comprehensiveness.  
4. There is no guidance on implementing LSRL in real-world applications, and the complexity of multi-label methods under the LSRL framework is not analyzed.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of the derivation of the general bound by providing more detailed and intuitive descriptions. Additionally, the authors should consider indicating bounds for a broader subset of network structures sharing common characteristics. To enhance comprehensiveness, we suggest expanding the theoretical analyses to include more label-specific representations. Furthermore, the authors should provide guidance on practical implementations of LSRL and analyze the computational complexity associated with the proposed framework.
