Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents CPGRec, a novel framework for video game recommendations that integrates category-based considerations and popularity guidance to balance accuracy and diversity. The authors propose three key components: an accuracy-driven module that enhances recommendation accuracy through stringent game connections, a diversity-driven module that connects neighbors across diverse categories, and a comprehensive module that employs negative-sample score reweighting. The evaluation on the Steam dataset demonstrates that CPGRec outperforms relevant baselines in both accuracy and diversity.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The paper addresses a significant problem in video game recommendations, making it relevant for the conference.
- The proposed framework effectively balances accuracy and diversity, showing improved results compared to existing methods.
- The inclusion of a sensitivity analysis and the provision of code enhance the comprehensibility and reproducibility of the work.

Weaknesses:
- There are ambiguities regarding the functionalities of the proposed modules and the definitions of evaluation metrics.
- The paper lacks comparisons with a broader range of diverse recommendation algorithms and does not utilize additional diversity metrics.
- The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, raising questions about the generalizability of the findings.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors clarify the distinctions between diversity and novelty/serendipity, particularly in relation to filter bubbles and echo chambers. Additionally, we suggest that the authors provide a more accurate definition of the coverage metric, aligning it with common research standards. It would be beneficial to include comparisons with more diverse recommendation algorithms and to utilize additional metrics for recommendation diversity, such as entropy. Finally, we encourage the authors to conduct experiments across multiple datasets to assess the robustness of their framework and to discuss the potential impact of their approach on user satisfaction.
