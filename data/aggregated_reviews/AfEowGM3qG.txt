Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a new task called NAMEGUESS, aimed at expanding abbreviated column names in tabular data, which is crucial for enhancing understandability and facilitating downstream algorithmic tasks. The authors propose NAMEGUESS as a natural language generation problem, distinguishing it from existing classification-style approaches. They provide a large-scale training dataset of 384,333 abbreviated-expanded column pairs and a human-annotated evaluation benchmark of 9,218 examples. The paper evaluates various language models (LMs) in both zero-shot and fine-tuned settings, analyzing their performance across different paradigms.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The significance of the NAMEGUESS task is well-motivated, addressing a pertinent issue in the intersection of NLP and database communities.
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with comprehensive analyses of results and ablation studies.
- The creation of a large-scale training set and a human-annotated benchmark demonstrates rigor and understanding of the challenges involved.

Weaknesses:
- The coverage of related work on profiling tables is limited, and the paper should better position its contributions within the existing literature.
- The task relies heavily on synthetic data generated through abbreviation lookups and probabilistic rules, which may not generalize well to real-world scenarios.
- Some decisions in the study, such as the consistency of abbreviations in the test datasets and the removal of hard examples, require further justification.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the positioning of their work by discussing related studies more comprehensively, particularly those addressing metadata profiling. Additionally, the authors should clarify the implications of using synthetic data for training and explore how their approach could be adapted to handle more complex abbreviation cases. Justifications for dataset design choices, particularly regarding the removal of difficult examples and adherence to abbreviation rules, should be provided to enhance the paper's robustness. Finally, we suggest including analyses of how column name expansion can significantly impact other NLP tasks, potentially through additional experiments.
