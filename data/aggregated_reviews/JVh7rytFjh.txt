Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a formal analysis of a sponsored QA platform that integrates organic answers with advertisements, utilizing a generalized second-price auction mechanism. The authors propose that advertisers bid on these sponsored answers, revealing key findings such as the incentivization of truthful bidding and the stability of the auction setting, which shares characteristics with VCG auctions. The analysis employs unigram language models as a foundational approach, highlighting that the auction winner may not always be the highest bidder and that user utility may not be maximized.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The topic of sponsored versus organic results in chat and RAG-based search engines is timely and relevant.
- The analysis serves as a foundational step in understanding the complexities of such systems.
- The concept of social welfare is intriguing and adds depth to the discussion.

Weaknesses:
- The theoretical findings lack practical implications and clarity regarding their significance for advertisers and the QA platform.
- The motivation for the theoretical framework is insufficiently articulated, and the necessity of various definitions and lemmas is unclear.
- Limited attention is given to the practical aspects from the advertisers' perspective and ethical considerations surrounding the proposed platform.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the clarity of their findings by providing simulations to illustrate the implications of non-maximal user utility. Additionally, the authors should strengthen the motivation for their theoretical framework and clarify the differences between their approach and existing methods like VCG. It would be beneficial to include a detailed example to aid comprehension and to address ethical considerations related to the proposed platform. Finally, we suggest that the authors refine the introduction to focus exclusively on QA platforms and better situate their work within the existing literature, particularly regarding recent studies on advertisement integration in chat contexts.
