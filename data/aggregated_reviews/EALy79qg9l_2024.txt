Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a novel framework for exploring the latent spaces of diffusion models through natural language prompts, significantly advancing traditional methods that rely on manual interpretation. The authors propose an automatic and scalable approach that enhances analysis without the need for specific vector training, allowing for a comprehensive understanding of semantic encoding in diffusion models. The framework has practical implications for identifying biases and improving AI model transparency, particularly in sensitive applications. Empirical evidence supports the framework's effectiveness in uncovering hidden patterns and biases.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:  
- The innovative framework enhances scalability and reduces manual effort in analyzing diffusion models.  
- It supports diverse analyses and provides practical applications for bias identification and transparency in AI.  
- Empirical validation demonstrates the framework's capability to reveal hidden patterns effectively.  
- The approach to investigating gender bias through one-to-one and one-to-many comparisons is intuitive and promising.  

Weaknesses:  
- Interpretation of latent space directions may remain complex for users lacking a strong background in machine learning or linguistics.  
- The framework's effectiveness is contingent on the accuracy and biases of the underlying language models, introducing potential bias.  
- Substantial computational resources may be required, limiting accessibility for some researchers.  
- Generalization across different generative models and domains has not been thoroughly discussed.  
- There is a risk of overfitting if natural language prompts lack diversity.  
- The paper could benefit from interventions on generated outputs to demonstrate practical applications of the framework.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the interpretability of latent space directions to assist users with varying expertise levels. Additionally, addressing the dependency on language model accuracy and exploring ways to reduce computational demands would enhance accessibility. We suggest including interventions on generated outputs, such as modifying gender representations in prompts, to demonstrate the framework's practical applications. Finally, we encourage the authors to investigate unexpected relationships within model internals that could have societal implications, beyond the expected biases observed.
