Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents Deep Relational Dependency Networks (Deep-RDN), a framework that integrates relational dependency networks and neural networks to process structured and unstructured data. It utilizes a decision tree for structured data and a neural network for unstructured inputs, incorporating domain knowledge through preference rules. The model is evaluated on ADE20k and RelKP, demonstrating superior performance over baselines in noisy, multimodal tasks.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- The novel combination of symbolic reasoning and deep learning shows consistent improvements over baselines, particularly in noisy environments.
- The evaluation is comprehensive, with clear metrics and effective integration of domain knowledge enhancing interpretability.

Weaknesses:
- The paper lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art decision deep networks and tree-based models.
- It does not address the logical penalty function's dependency on unstructured data, which could invalidate its effectiveness.
- The balance between contributions from neural refinement and the relational probabilistic tree (RPT) remains unclear.
- The choice of dataset, ADE20k, is primarily unstructured and may not be suitable for high-stakes applications like healthcare.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the paper by conducting experiments on large-scale datasets and comparing their method with state-of-the-art models. Additionally, clarifying the role of the logical penalty function in relation to unstructured data, addressing how to balance contributions from neural refinement and RPT, and exploring the model's performance under varying noise levels in structured data would strengthen the analysis. Finally, utilizing more relevant datasets, such as MIMIC-IV or CheXpert, would better validate the model's applicability in healthcare contexts.
