Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a method for predicting mutation classes of quivers using graph neural networks. The authors demonstrate that models trained on quivers with 6 to 10 nodes can accurately predict classes for quivers with 11 nodes. They analyze the latent space embedding of the models, revealing that it reflects known typologies of quiver classes, indicating that the models have learned significant mathematical concepts. Additionally, the paper leverages DirGINE for predicting quiver mutation equivalence, with visualizations that highlight the practical value of the approach.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:  
- The application of machine learning to a relevant problem in graph theory is innovative and noteworthy.  
- High accuracy in predicting quiver classes for larger nodes demonstrates the effectiveness of the models.  
- The theoretical analysis and case studies are extensive, contributing to the paper's depth.  

Weaknesses:  
- The paper lacks quantitative results that could provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.  
- There is a need for further investigation using more expressive GNN variants and advanced GNN explainability techniques.  

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the paper by adding quantitative results and discussing them in the main text to enhance reader comprehension. Additionally, we suggest exploring more expressive GNN variants and incorporating state-of-the-art GNN explainability techniques to strengthen the analysis.
