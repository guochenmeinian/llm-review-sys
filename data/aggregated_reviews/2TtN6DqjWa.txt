Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents a data augmentation framework for learning interpretable style embeddings, utilizing GPT-3 to generate natural language descriptions of writing styles based on a set of 87 prompts. The resulting dataset, termed "StyleGenome," is processed into interpretable stylistic attributes. The authors propose "LISA Style Vectors," created from these attributes, and evaluate their performance against existing style embeddings using an evaluation framework from Wegmann and Nguyen (2021). The findings indicate that LISA embeddings are competitive with recent alternatives while offering superior interpretability.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- Creative use of LLMs to generate a new dataset with style attribute annotations.
- The distillation framework effectively transfers knowledge from GPT-3 to a smaller, more efficient model.
- Comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons with human judgments and other recent style embedding approaches.
- Commitment to sharing the dataset and models with the community.

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient investigation into the quality of the StyleGenome dataset, raising concerns about potential noise from LLM outputs.
- Lack of clarity on the feedback guiding manual decisions in the pipeline, such as prompt design and filtering.
- Uncertainty regarding the distinctiveness of stylistic attributes, as SBERT similarity may not capture all near-duplicate cases.
- The mapping from open-set natural language attributes to a fixed set of features is difficult to understand.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the evaluation of the StyleGenome dataset to better assess the generalization of individual attributes and address potential noise issues. Additionally, providing clarity on the feedback mechanisms used for manual decisions in the pipeline would strengthen the methodology. We suggest exploring more standardized approaches, such as TFIDF, for attribute selection to enhance robustness. Improving the presentation of the mapping from natural language attributes to fixed features could also facilitate understanding. Finally, comparing different versions of StyleGenome using various LLMs, particularly open-source ones, would provide valuable insights.
