Aggregated Review:
### Key Points
This paper presents an innovative 2D hairstyle editing pipeline utilizing latent diffusion models (LDMs) and introduces a novel approach to multi-color hairstyle transfer through a warp model that has not been previously applied in this domain. The core of this method is the Multi-Stage Hairstyle Blend (MHB), which allows for distinct control over hairstyle and hair color while effectively preserving non-hair attributes by incorporating structural information from non-hair regions, such as facial masks and keypoints. A hair warping module facilitates the natural transfer of hair color from the source to the target image. Extensive experiments on datasets like CelebA-HQ demonstrate that the proposed method yields superior results compared to previous state-of-the-art techniques, particularly in the multi-color task, as validated through ablation studies and comparative evaluations. The authors also address ethical concerns regarding data collection, confirming that their user studies have received IRB approval and that images used have been processed to remove personally identifiable information.

### Strengths and Weaknesses
Strengths:
- Clarity and Writing: The paper is well-written and relatively easy to understand.
- Technical Contributions: The submission includes notable contributions, such as the hair warping module and the MHB module, which effectively preserve non-hair attributes.
- Extensive Experiments: The method produces more natural hair editing results, maintains consistent hair color with input sources, and is validated through user studies and ablation studies.
- Innovative Application: The application of the warp model in hairstyle color transfer is a significant contribution.
- Ethical Considerations: Ethical concerns regarding data collection and processing are acknowledged and addressed.

Weaknesses:
- Limited Discussion of Failure Cases: The results primarily showcase near-frontal head poses, lacking diversity in pose representation. More discussions on failure cases, particularly with color transfer between different hairstyles, are needed.
- Manuscript Quality: The paper contains typos and missing text, necessitating thorough proofreading.
- Clarity of Technical Details: Some notations are confusing, and the ablation study lacks clarity and detail. The user study is poorly described, lacking specifics about the questions and domains involved.
- Novelty Concerns: There are concerns about the overall novelty of the work, particularly regarding claims of addressing paired datasets and preserving hairstyle structure.
- Overstated Claims: The assertion of better preservation of irrelevant attributes in Figure 4 may be overstated, raising questions about the method's actual improvements over existing techniques.

### Suggestions for Improvement
We recommend that the authors improve the discussion on failure cases by including results with non-frontal head poses and addressing limitations in color transfer between different hairstyles. Additionally, a thorough proofreading of the manuscript is necessary to correct typos and enhance clarity. It would also be beneficial to clarify confusing notations and provide more detailed descriptions of the ablation study and user study methodologies. Furthermore, we suggest that the authors refine their presentation of results in Figure 4 to accurately reflect the preservation of irrelevant attributes, ensuring that claims are substantiated by the data presented. Finally, providing a more detailed comparison with prior methods that utilized similar approaches would strengthen the claims regarding the novelty and effectiveness of their method.
